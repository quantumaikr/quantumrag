# EU AI Act: Risk Classifications

## Four Risk Levels

### 1. Unacceptable Risk (Prohibited)

Eight AI application types incompatible with EU values and fundamental rights:

1. **Subliminal manipulation** — Changing behavior without awareness causing harm (e.g., influencing voting without consent)
2. **Exploitation of vulnerabilities** — Targeting social/economic situation, age, or physical/mental ability (e.g., voice assistants encouraging children toward dangerous activities)
3. **Biometric categorization based on sensitive characteristics** — Gender, ethnicity, political orientation, religion, sexual orientation, philosophical beliefs
4. **General purpose social scoring** — Rating individuals on personal characteristics, behavior, and activities without justified connection to decisions
5. **Real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces** — Completely banned except law enforcement with judicial approval for targeted searches of crime victims, terrorism prevention, or serious criminals
6. **Emotional state assessment** — At workplace or in education (exceptions: safety purposes like detecting drowsy drivers)
7. **Predictive policing** — Assessing crime risk based on personal traits
8. **Facial image scraping** — Creating/expanding databases through untargeted collection from internet or surveillance footage

### 2. High-Risk AI Systems

Safety components of regulated products and stand-alone systems potentially causing significant harm.

**Annex III Categories (8 areas):**
- (a) Biometric and biometrics-based systems
- (b) Critical infrastructure management (road traffic, energy, digital infrastructure)
- (c) Education and vocational training
- (d) Employment and worker management (recruitment, performance evaluation, task allocation)
- (e) Essential services/benefits access (credit-scoring, health insurance risk, emergency dispatch)
- (f) Law enforcement (evidence reliability, crime analytics)
- (g) Migration, asylum, border control (security risk assessment, visa/permit examination)
- (h) Justice and democratic processes (fact/law interpretation, election influence)

**Requirements**: Risk management, human oversight, robustness, accuracy, data quality, cybersecurity, technical documentation, transparency. EU maintains a public register of deployed high-risk systems.

### 3. Limited Risk (Transparency Required)

AI systems with manipulation or deceit risk. Humans must be informed of AI interaction unless obvious. Deepfakes must be labeled.

Examples: Chatbots and generative AI systems.

### 4. Minimal Risk (No Restrictions)

All other AI systems. Examples: Spam filters, AI-enabled video games.

Recommendation: Voluntary adherence to general principles — human oversight, non-discrimination, fairness.

## GPAI (General Purpose AI) Requirements

Two risk classes based on computing power for training:

- **Non-systemic risk** — Transparency requirements only
- **Systemic risk** — Stricter obligations; creators must provide information to downstream high-risk application providers

**Open-source exception**: Publicly available models with open licenses can avoid stricter requirements if unrelated to prohibited/high-risk applications.

## Penalty Structure

| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| Prohibited practices (Article 5) | Up to €35 million or 7% of global annual turnover |
| High-risk system violations | Up to €15 million or 3% of global annual turnover |
| Providing incorrect information | Up to €7.5 million or 1% of global annual turnover |

For SMEs, the fine is capped at the lower of the two amounts.

## Implementation Timeline

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| August 2024 | AI Act enters into force |
| February 2025 | Ban on unacceptable-risk systems effective |
| August 2025 | GPAI transparency requirements + AI Office enforcement |
| February 2026 | Commission publishes high-risk use case guidelines |
| August 2026 | Full enforcement of all provisions including high-risk |

## Generative AI Transparency Standards

Generative systems (ChatGPT-type models) must:
- Disclose AI-generated content origin
- Prevent illegal content generation through design
- Publish copyrighted training data summaries

High-impact general-purpose models like GPT-4 require thorough evaluations, and any serious incidents must be reported to the European Commission.
